The theory of plate tectonics is a theory that was tried to be proved by a man named Alfred Wegener. In the 1900's, people believed that he was crazy and had lost his mind, like all other people who tried to prove an excellent theory. Plate tectonics is the theory that the outer rigid layer of the earth (the lithosphere) is divided into a couple of dozen "plates" that move around across the earth's surface relative to each other, like slabs of ice on a lake.

Convergent collisional faults occur when two plates of continental crust collide with each other. It usually forms a mountain.

Convergent subductional boundaries are like convergent collisional except one plate goes under the other. This usually forms and volcano.

Divergent boundaries move away from each other. They allow lava to escape from the mantle. That lava cools and forms new crust.

Transform boundaries move harmlessly next to each other. This sometimes causes earthquakes to happen at these boundaries.
